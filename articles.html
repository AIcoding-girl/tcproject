Tennis is a very popular sport in Europe and is played by people of all ages. Tennis can be played as a sport or as a recreational activity with friends and family. Either way, playing tennis is a good sport to maintain your health, fitness, strength and agility. It has been calculated that an hour-long game of singles tennis burns around 600 calories for men and 420 calories for women.

In addition to the fitness and physical health benefits, tennis also provides numerous social and mental health benefits as well:

  • a non-impact sport;
  • a great way to meet people and spend time with friends;
  • suitable for all ages and skill levels. You can find someone of a similar ability to play with;
  • helpful to reduce stress;
  • not dependent on youth or strength – you can play for a lifetime or start the game at any age;

Social wellness involves engaging in and enjoying positive interactions with other people in work and leisure and building and maintaining meaningful friendships, intimate relationships and professional connections.

Here are a few suggested activities that will support you in cultivating social wellness—use these as a starting point to inspire your own ideas! We offer the sport and social experience!

  • Go to the gym, the park, or a yoga, fitness or dance class in our centre
  • Plan a regular lunch date with your family
  • Organize a spa day with a group of friends or colleagues

Practice with a purpose, play with a passion!

Tennis is played in organised competitions as well as social games. In order to play tennis you need a racquet, a ball, a court with a net, and an opponent. Club courts and public courts are available for hire in most suburbs and towns. If you can’t find someone to play against, we host organised social play, competitions and tournaments that will provide fellow tennis players for you to play with and against.
